ArtSmart was founded by operatic tenors Michael Fabiano and John Viscardi, who met at the Academy of Vocal Arts in Philadelphia and have been friends for over a decade. ArtSmart begins each year with auditions for students interested in the program. Those who are accepted are paired with a mentor. ArtSmart’s success is largely due to students building strong, positive relationships with adult role models, allowing them to use artistic expression to communicate freely within that connection. The program equips students with essential skills, such as setting and achieving goals, navigating challenges healthily, and gaining the self-confidence to advocate for their beliefs – and they have the data to prove that it works.

In June 2023, ArtSmart concluded a two-year longitudinal study in partnership with the WCG Institutional Review Board to evaluate its impact on student social, emotional, and academic outcomes. Key findings show that ArtSmart has a positive, causal impact on students that goes well beyond music education. Participation in ArtSmart significantly boosts students’ intentions to pursue higher education, nurtures meaningful connections with adults at school, provides students with an adult they can talk to when stressed or having problems, and is a driving factor in excitement to attend school. 

Through these comprehensive benefits, ArtSmart creates a ripple effect of positive change for individuals and communities by equipping youth with the skills, confidence, relationships, and ambition to succeed in all walks of life.
